Cardio Program

Description

By definition, cardio workouts can be any exercise—jogging, running, biking, swimming, elliptical machine, stairs, battling ropes even jumping rope—that raises and maintains your heart rate over a predetermined amount of time.

Cardio workouts burn fat. And cardio fitness is what gives you endurance and the ability to persist in sports and in life. An example workout could consists of running 10 - 40 yard sprints.

Toning program

Description

Getting toned means that you are building muscle and losing the layer of fat over these muscles, so that you achieve a healthy body shape.

This means that to get toned, your workouts should be a combination of strength training to build lean muscle mass, and cardio to lose fat. The best way to achieve this goal is through Interval Training or H.I.I.T. (High Intensity Interval Training).

An example of this type of training would be Tabata Training. Simply :20 of squats followed by :10 of rest, that’s 1 round and you want to do this 8 times.

Functionality program

Description

Functional training is a classification of exercise which involves training the body for the activities performed in daily life.

Functional training, if performed correctly, will lead to better joint mobility and stability, as well as more efficient motor patterns. The key to functional exercise is about teaching all the muscles to work together rather than isolating them to work independently. An example of a Functional exercise would be the DB Squat and Press overhead.
